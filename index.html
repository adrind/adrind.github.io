Code for America

In 2017 I accepted a nine month long fellowship with Code for America to work on developing digital solutions for our country's most pressing problems. Working on a team of two, we were assigned to Anchorage, Alaska to build a tool that would help unemployed residents connect to the public workforce system. In nine months I developed five working prototypes that we tested with users before settling on shipping two websites: StartHereAnchorage and ResumeGuide.me.

Fitbit

I joined the Device Cornerstone team at Fitbit in 2014. We were responsible for the client applications that would sync data from the devices to our servers. I worked on the desktop client application to support six device launches: Charge HR, Surge, Blaze, Charge 2, Alta, and Flex 2. I moved to the Web Platform team after we decided to rewrite our website into a new Ember.js application. As part of the founding team, we successfully launched Fitbit's first Ember.js web application.

LinkedIn

My first job out of college was as an Associate Web Developer on the new LinkedIn Sales Navigator project. We built the website from the ground up as a new service using the Play framework in Scala and Backbone.js on the frontend.
